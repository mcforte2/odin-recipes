Classic Goulash

First up, we have a classic one-dish meal: American goulash. Not to be confused with Hungarian goulash, American goulash is made with easy to find, budget-friendly ingredients including pasta, ground beef, tomato sauce, and diced tomatoes. "I grew up on this in Iowa..." says reviewer Leanie A..

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

  • 1 large yellow onion, chopped

  • 2 pounds lean ground beef

  • 3 cloves garlic, chopped

  • 3 cups water

  • 2 (15 ounce) cans tomato sauce

  • 2 (14.5 ounce) cans diced tomatoes

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce

  • 2 tablespoons dried Italian herb seasoning

  • 1 tablespoon seasoned salt, or to taste

  • 3 bay leaves

  • 2 cups uncooked elbow macaroni

Directions

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook 3 minutes, stirring often.
  3. Add ground beef and cook, breaking up the meat until it is evenly browned, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook 3 minutes, stirring often.
  4. seasoned salt, and bay leaves. Bring to a simmer, cover and cook 5 minutes, stirring halfway through.
  5. Stir in uncooked macaroni, cover, and simmer over medium heat until pasta is tender, 11 to 13 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Remove from heat, discard bay leaves, and serve.
